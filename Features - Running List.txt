6. Email invoice button
Great idea, very viable. My take:
Pros: Removes the PDF-print-then-email manual step entirely. Auto-Sent status is the killer feature here — it's one less click and keeps the workflow tight.
Architecture I'd recommend: Use Python's smtplib with a Gmail app password (or a Mailgun/Postmark free tier for reliability). The print template already renders the full invoice as HTML — you can send that directly as an HTML email body, no PDF generation needed. Attach nothing; the email is the invoice.
UX: The button is btn-outline-primary with a mail icon, greyed/disabled if bill_to_email is empty. Clicking opens a small confirmation modal showing the recipient address, subject line preview (e.g. "Invoice 2025_124 — Handyman Services, June 2025"), and a Send button. On success: status auto-moves to Sent, toast confirms, sent_at is stamped.
One consideration: You'll need somewhere to store SMTP credentials (not in the repo). A .env file that's already in .gitignore is the standard approach. Worth building — it's maybe 40 lines of Python and a modal.
